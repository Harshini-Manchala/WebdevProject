History of INDIAN FOREST

  • 1906
    Imperial Forest Research Institute was established.
  • 1916
    The Indian Home Rule movement
    The Non-cooperation movement both challenged British rule in India. There was a general disregard for forest laws and forest damage by fires and unnecessary felling in Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Orissa and Andhra Pradesh in protest against British rule.
  • 1927
    Indian Forest Ac
    It was passed mainly to consolidate the existing laws related to forestry.
  • 1952
    Second Forest Policy
    The forest policy stipulated that 1/ 3rd of the country's total land area shoud be kept under forest, with a percentage of 60% in mountainous regions.
  • 1968
    Westoby coined “Social Forestry”
    It wa held at World Forest Congress held in India.
  • 1972
    Wildlife (Protection) Act
    It was passed for preserving and protecting wildlife in India
  • 1973
    Chipko movement
    It was started by Sunderlal Bahughuna in 1973 in Mandal village of Uttarakhand against government giving a forest land to a sport good company.
  • 1980
    Forest Conservation Act
  • 1981
    Indian Institute of Forest management
    It was established in Bhopal.
  • 1986
    Environment (Protection) Act
  • 1987
    Indian Council of Forestry Research and Education (ICFRE)
    It was started in Dehradun
  • 1988
    Third Forest Policy
  • 2002
    Biodiversity Act
    National Afforestation Programme (NAP)
  • 2006
    Forest Rights Act
  • 2014
    National Agroforestry Policy
